1. Founder & Startup Background

I’m Mahammad Saddam Hussain, the founder of Xpieder. The idea was born from observing a paradox — we live in a hyper-connected world, yet real human connection feels increasingly scarce. Before this, I ran tech-led ventures in education and retail, but I always felt something was missing in how we connect.

The personal trigger? Watching people share the same physical space, like a café or college campus, but being entirely consumed by digital feeds. I envisioned a world where our environments could talk back — not through screens, but through space itself. That seed led to Xpieder.

2. Startup Overview

Xpieder stands for "Experience + Spider" — mapping experiences like a web across physical spaces.

Founded in 2024 in Assam, Xpieder is a spatial social platform using Augmented Reality to enable people to drop, discover, and interact with digital content anchored in real-world locations.

Our core mission is simple: To bring presence back to social connection.

5. Problem & Solution

The problem: Gen Z is the most connected generation, yet the loneliest.

Today’s platforms prioritize engagement over presence. Xpieder flips that—by turning AR into a medium for social interaction, not just a feature.

You don’t scroll here. You explore. Connections unfold outdoors, organically, tied to space and time.

6. Market

Our early adopters are urban Gen Z and college students — the ones craving meaningful connections beyond feed culture.

The AR market is projected to surpass $100B by 2030. Within this, spatial social layers are an emerging category, and we’re pioneering that space.

User acquisition starts hyperlocal: we seed content in campuses, parks, and cafés to create an explorative loop.

7. Business Model

We’re exploring two models:

  • Freemium – with spatial upgrades and exclusive AR content.
  • B2B2C – Businesses can pin live offers, events, or experiences at physical locations to drive real-world engagement.

Highly scalable through geographic and interest-based expansion.

10. Competition & Challenges

While apps like Snapchat and Niantic play with AR, they use it as an engagement tool.

Xpieder is different: we treat AR as a communication medium.

Biggest challenge? Educating users that the future of social isn’t virtual — it’s spatial.

11. Lessons Learned

Early hurdle: Building a networked product where experience depends on place.

Key lesson: Preload the world with content. Make it feel alive, even for the first user.

12. Future Plans

Short-term: Expand pilot zones and refine AR experiences.

Long-term: Launch in multiple Indian metros, integrate wearables, and build toward a fully immersive XR platform.

13. Impact & Recognition

Xpieder redefines how people connect, explore, and express. By encouraging real-world engagement, we’re addressing digital isolation head-on.

We’ve been spotlighted by regional innovation platforms and awarded under government-backed startup programs.

14. Advice for Aspiring Founders

Solve a problem you feel deeply.

Build for the world ahead, but root yourself in today’s realities.

Most importantly — build presence, not just product.
